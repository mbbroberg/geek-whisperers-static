<\/a>We’re pleased to bring you a conversation with William Lam<\/strong>,\u00a0Staff Engineer II working within the VMware R&D organization and creator of the ever-popular VirtuallyGhetto.com<\/a>. The Whisperers spoke with William back at VMworld, and due to a number of lost files and a miraculous backup copy, we now deliver it to you.<\/p>\n

We discuss:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Joining R&D\u00a0from the customer side of the story<\/li>\n
  • The transition from TME to R&D Engineering<\/li>\n
  • Filtering content and serving it up to the customer<\/li>\n
  • Feeling closer to the customer by playing with the software<\/li>\n
  • His role is diverse: “today could be VSAN, tomorrow could be NSX”<\/li>\n
  • Starting early in blogging<\/li>\n
  • Doubting the value of his blog (he didn’t\u00a0believe people would read his blog)<\/li>\n
  • The naming of Virtually Ghetto and its humble beginnings<\/li>\n
  • Advocating for the customer within the company<\/li>\n
  • Not worrying about politics or swim lanes, but doing the best work for the customer<\/li>\n
  • Having engineers who wrote the code troubleshoot using your blog<\/li>\n
  • Answering the questions: what are the use cases, how is it getting used, how do you fix it?<\/li>\n
  • Providing feedback from customer back into engineering as well<\/li>\n
  • Working for the enterprise versus playing in the startup lands<\/li>\n
  • How’d you get here? Honestly no clue<\/li>\n
  • Safe is a relative idea that doesn’t always make good or bad<\/li>\n
  • We all have non-linear paths to the roles we get to<\/li>\n
  • Do the work and share it publicly<\/li>\n
  • Career planning is not his favorite thing – keep up with the passion<\/li>\n
  • One takeaway: persevere<\/strong>. It will show<\/li>\n
  • Gut versus metrics? He’s a gut guy<\/li>\n
  • Fulfill the commitment and keep going forward<\/li>\n
  • Don’t run away from the fear – there’s good from it<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    You can follow William on Twitter @lamw<\/a> and on his incredible blog, Virtually Ghetto<\/a>. Thanks for this episode and thanks for the backup copy Mark<\/a>!<\/p>\n
