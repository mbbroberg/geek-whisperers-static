Prescribed tweets are a method of sharing prewritten\u00a0content that a colleague (most often marketing) requests\u00a0you send\u00a0(most often\u00a0verbatim).<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

Cons:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Duplicate tweets on the same hashtag looks inauthentic<\/li>\n
  • Harms your own brand if you use it<\/li>\n
  • Minimal effort to add your own edits to it<\/li>\n
  • You have to offer your own reaction or you harm it<\/li>\n
  • Maximizing\u00a0number of tweets is wasteful<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Pros:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Prescription is bad, reframe it as suggested tweets<\/li>\n
    • Help those less native to the social media platform<\/li>\n
    • Help those without the time to consume it all (see Don’t Make Me Think<\/a>)<\/li>\n
    • Great method of internal notification<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Recommendations:<\/p>\n

        \n
      • Share links internally like you\u00a0share\u00a0externally<\/li>\n
      • Help people know how to share it through frameworks, not prescription<\/li>\n
      • Keep the formatting simple when you share<\/li>\n
      • Prevent copy\/paste-able tweets to avoid bad behavior<\/li>\n
      • TL;DR – give people training wheels to improve<\/li>\n
      • Consistent sharing is incredibly effective – get people hooked<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
