How many emails is too many emails when you’re talking to a recruiter? And how do they get paid? We answer these\u00a0questions and many more this week with Liz Bronson. Liz is a recruiting consultant and was the recruiter during the early years of VMware. Her business focuses on the crazy portfolio of technology companies we know and love, so it’s a special opportunity to get her advice. She takes some time with us to fill in the gaps and refresh all our memory on how to be a great job candidate. Some highlights:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Before actively job hunting, do some soul searching<\/li>\n
  • Good to keep passively job hunting too<\/li>\n
  • Be prepared for an initial conversation (ex. look at the freaking website)<\/li>\n
  • 3 major types of recruiters: Internal, Retained Search, Contingent<\/li>\n
  • Know how they’re paid<\/li>\n
  • How a recruiter works to prompt a hiring manager<\/li>\n
  • How often is too often to ping a recruiter? (Recommendation to check-in once a week)<\/li>\n
  • Remember to be respectful to the recruiter. She is the gatekeeper<\/li>\n
  • What do recruiters have a say or not?<\/li>\n
  • Best advice of the whole show:\u00a0the first offer you get is not the best and final offer<\/li>\n
  • Truck of money jobs will not make you happy for very long<\/li>\n
  • “Nobody’s life changes more than yours when you take a new job.” Ask the questions you want answered<\/li>\n
  • Don’t ever complain about your past job. You’re making a first impression, keep it positive<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
